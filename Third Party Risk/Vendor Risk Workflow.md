# Vendor Risk Workflow

## Overview

The Vendor Risk Workflow establishes the governance process used to identify, assess, monitor, remediate, and govern third-party risks throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of this workflow is to:

- Reduce third-party risk exposure
- Improve vendor governance oversight
- Support regulatory compliance requirements
- Strengthen supply chain security
- Improve executive visibility into vendor risk
- Enhance audit readiness
- Standardize vendor assessment and monitoring activities

This workflow applies to all vendors, suppliers, service providers, contractors, partners, and third parties that provide products, services, technology, or access to organizational assets and data.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Third parties introduce risk.

Organizations often extend access to:

- Sensitive information
- Business systems
- Customer data
- Critical business processes
- Technology infrastructure

While third parties provide business value, they also introduce:

- Security risk
- Operational risk
- Compliance risk
- Financial risk
- Reputational risk

Vendor governance exists to ensure third-party relationships remain aligned with organizational risk tolerance and regulatory obligations.

---

# Vendor Risk Lifecycle

All vendors should follow a standardized governance lifecycle.

```text
Vendor Identification
          ↓
Vendor Classification
          ↓
Inherent Risk Assessment
          ↓
Security Assessment
          ↓
Risk Review
          ↓
Approval Decision
          ↓
Vendor Onboarding
          ↓
Continuous Monitoring
          ↓
Periodic Reassessment
          ↓
Offboarding
```

---

# Vendor Governance Objectives

The Vendor Risk Program seeks to:

- Identify vendor risk exposure
- Classify vendors consistently
- Assess vendor security posture
- Monitor ongoing vendor risk
- Track remediation activities
- Support regulatory compliance
- Improve governance reporting
- Reduce supply chain risk

---

# Vendor Assessment Triggers

Vendor assessments should occur when:

- New vendor onboarding
- Contract renewal
- Significant service change
- Security incident involving vendor
- Regulatory requirement changes
- Annual vendor review
- Executive request
- Acquisition or merger activity

---

# Vendor Classification Process

Every vendor should be classified before onboarding.

Classification should consider:

- Business dependency
- Data sensitivity
- System access
- Regulatory exposure
- Operational importance
- Service criticality

Classification determines:

- Assessment depth
- Review frequency
- Monitoring requirements
- Approval authority

The Vendor Tiering Model should be used for formal classification.

---

# Inherent Risk Assessment

An inherent risk assessment evaluates vendor risk before considering existing controls.

Assessment areas include:

## Data Exposure

Questions:

- Does the vendor access sensitive data?
- Does the vendor store regulated information?
- Does the vendor process customer data?

Examples:

- PII
- PHI
- Financial data
- Intellectual property

---

## System Access

Questions:

- Does the vendor access internal systems?
- Does the vendor have privileged access?
- Does the vendor integrate with production systems?

Examples:

- VPN access
- Administrative access
- API integrations

---

## Operational Dependency

Questions:

- Does the organization depend on the vendor?
- Would service disruption impact operations?

Examples:

- Critical SaaS providers
- Cloud providers
- Payroll providers

---

## Regulatory Exposure

Questions:

- Does the vendor support regulated activities?
- Does the vendor impact compliance obligations?

Examples:

- HIPAA
- SOX
- PCI DSS
- ISO 27001
- NIST-based programs

---

# Security Assessment Requirements

Security reviews should evaluate:

- Security governance
- Access controls
- Identity management
- Vulnerability management
- Incident response
- Data protection
- Business continuity
- Regulatory compliance

Evidence may include:

- SOC Reports
- ISO Certifications
- Security Policies
- Penetration Test Reports
- Risk Assessments
- Security Questionnaires

---

# Risk Scoring Methodology

Vendor risks should be scored using the organization's Risk Scoring Methodology.

Assessment dimensions:

- Likelihood
- Business Impact
- Regulatory Exposure
- Control Maturity

Results should be documented in the Vendor Risk Register.

---

# Vendor Approval Process

After assessment, vendors should receive one of four decisions.

| Decision | Description |
|---|---|
| Approved | Risk within acceptable tolerance |
| Conditionally Approved | Approved with required remediation |
| Risk Acceptance Required | Business accepts identified risks |
| Rejected | Risk exceeds organizational tolerance |

Approval decisions should be documented and retained.

---

# Vendor Onboarding Requirements

Before onboarding:

- Assessment completed
- Risks documented
- Required approvals obtained
- Contracts reviewed
- Security requirements established
- Regulatory obligations documented

No vendor should be onboarded without completing governance review requirements.

---

# Continuous Monitoring

Approved vendors should remain under ongoing governance monitoring.

Monitoring activities may include:

- Security incident reviews
- Compliance reviews
- Risk reassessments
- Contract reviews
- Performance monitoring
- News and threat monitoring
- Regulatory monitoring

Continuous monitoring helps identify changing risk conditions.

---

# Vendor Remediation Management

When deficiencies are identified:

1. Document finding
2. Assign remediation owner
3. Establish remediation timeline
4. Track progress
5. Validate completion

Vendor remediation activities should follow organizational Issue Management processes.

---

# Vendor Reassessment Requirements

Periodic reassessments should evaluate:

- Current risk posture
- New regulatory obligations
- Service changes
- Security incidents
- Control effectiveness
- Organizational dependency changes

Reassessment frequency should align with vendor tier and risk level.

---

# Vendor Offboarding Process

When vendor relationships end:

- Remove access
- Recover assets
- Validate data disposition
- Review contractual obligations
- Close governance records
- Update vendor inventory

Offboarding activities should be documented.

---

# Escalation Requirements

Vendor risks should be escalated when:

- Risk exceeds tolerance
- Regulatory exposure increases
- Security incidents occur
- Remediation deadlines are missed
- Critical vendors fail assessments

Escalation should follow the Escalation Procedures Framework.

---

# Governance Reporting

Vendor reporting should provide visibility into:

- Active vendors
- High-risk vendors
- Critical vendors
- Open vendor findings
- Vendor remediation status
- Risk acceptance decisions
- Assessment completion rates
- Vendor risk trends

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Recommended vendor risk metrics include:

| KPI | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Active Vendor Count | Measures vendor inventory |
| High-Risk Vendor Count | Measures third-party exposure |
| Assessment Completion Rate | Measures governance effectiveness |
| Open Vendor Findings | Measures remediation workload |
| Vendor Remediation Completion Rate | Measures risk reduction effectiveness |
| Vendor Risk Acceptance Count | Measures accepted exposure |

---

# Governance Review Frequency

| Vendor Risk Level | Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Low | Annual |
| Moderate | Semi-Annual |
| High | Quarterly |
| Critical | Continuous Monitoring + Quarterly Review |

---

# Audit Readiness Considerations

The Vendor Risk Program should maintain evidence of:

- Vendor inventories
- Risk assessments
- Security reviews
- Approval decisions
- Monitoring activities
- Remediation efforts
- Risk acceptance decisions
- Offboarding records

Documentation should be retained according to evidence retention requirements.

# Summary

The Vendor Risk Workflow establishes a structured governance process for managing third-party risk throughout the vendor lifecycle.

By enforcing vendor classification, risk assessments, security reviews, remediation tracking, continuous monitoring, and governance reporting, the organization strengthens supply chain security, regulatory compliance, audit readiness, and enterprise risk management maturity.
