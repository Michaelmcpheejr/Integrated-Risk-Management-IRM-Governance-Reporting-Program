# Vendor Risk Acceptance Process

## Overview

The Vendor Risk Acceptance Process establishes the governance framework used to formally evaluate, document, approve, monitor, and review third-party risks that cannot be fully remediated but are determined to be acceptable to the organization.

The purpose of this process is to:

- Support informed risk-based decision making
- Establish accountability for accepted risks
- Improve executive visibility into third-party exposure
- Strengthen governance oversight
- Support regulatory compliance obligations
- Improve audit readiness
- Ensure accepted risks remain within organizational risk tolerance

Risk acceptance is a governance decision, not a remediation activity.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Not all vendor risks can be eliminated.

Examples include:

- Legacy vendor platforms
- Industry-standard control limitations
- Business-critical vendors with known deficiencies
- Vendor remediation timelines extending beyond business requirements
- Operational dependencies that cannot be immediately replaced

In these situations, leadership may determine that accepting risk creates less organizational impact than rejecting the vendor relationship.

Risk acceptance should never occur by default.

All accepted risks must be:

- Documented
- Assessed
- Approved
- Monitored
- Periodically reviewed

---

# Risk Acceptance Principles

The Vendor Risk Acceptance Program operates using the following principles.

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| Formal Approval Required | Accepted risks require documented approval. |
| Risk-Based Decision Making | Acceptance decisions must be supported by risk analysis. |
| Executive Visibility | Significant accepted risks require leadership awareness. |
| Temporary by Design | Accepted risks should be reviewed periodically. |
| Accountability | Every accepted risk must have an owner. |
| Audit Traceability | Acceptance decisions must be documented and retained. |

---

# Risk Acceptance Lifecycle

All vendor risk acceptance requests should follow a standardized governance workflow.

```text
Vendor Risk Identified
          ↓
Risk Assessment
          ↓
Remediation Evaluation
          ↓
Compensating Control Review
          ↓
Risk Acceptance Request
          ↓
Approval Review
          ↓
Risk Acceptance Decision
          ↓
Monitoring
          ↓
Periodic Review
          ↓
Renewal or Closure
```

---

# Risk Acceptance Eligibility

Risk acceptance should only be considered when:

- Remediation is not feasible
- Business dependency is significant
- Vendor replacement is impractical
- Risk remains within organizational tolerance
- Compensating controls exist
- Leadership understands the exposure

Risk acceptance should not be used to avoid remediation responsibilities.

---

# Common Vendor Risk Acceptance Scenarios

Examples include:

- Vendor lacks a desired security certification
- Vendor cannot support MFA for a legacy platform
- Vendor requires extended remediation timelines
- Vendor provides critical business services despite known deficiencies
- Industry limitations prevent full compliance

---

# Required Risk Acceptance Documentation

Every request should include:

- Risk Acceptance ID
- Vendor Name
- Vendor Tier
- Risk Description
- Risk Score
- Control Maturity Assessment
- Business Justification
- Compensating Controls
- Risk Owner
- Approval Authority
- Review Date
- Expiration Date

Incomplete requests should not proceed for approval.

---

# Risk Assessment Requirements

All acceptance requests must be supported by a documented risk assessment.

Assessment factors include:

- Likelihood
- Business Impact
- Regulatory Exposure
- Control Maturity
- Asset Criticality
- Vendor Criticality

The organization's Risk Scoring Methodology should be used.

---

# Compensating Controls

Compensating controls should be implemented whenever practical.

Examples:

| Risk | Compensating Control |
|---|---|
| Vendor lacks MFA | Enhanced monitoring and quarterly access reviews |
| Missing certification | Independent assessment and annual evidence review |
| Delayed remediation | Increased monitoring and executive reporting |
| Limited logging capability | Additional internal monitoring controls |

Compensating controls should reduce risk exposure while the accepted risk remains active.

---

# Approval Authority Matrix

Approval requirements are determined by risk severity.

| Risk Severity | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Low | Department Manager |
| Moderate | Director |
| High | Executive Leadership |
| Regulatory Exposure Present | Executive Leadership + Compliance Review |

---

# Executive Risk Acceptance

Executive approval is required when:

- High-risk vendors are involved
- Regulatory exposure exists
- Critical vendors are affected
- Significant business dependency exists
- Risk exceeds normal management authority

Executive approval demonstrates organizational awareness and acceptance of residual exposure.

---

# Regulatory Review Requirements

Additional review should occur when accepted risks affect:

- HIPAA
- SOX
- PCI DSS
- ISO 27001
- NIST Framework Requirements
- Customer Contracts
- Legal Obligations

Compliance and Legal stakeholders should participate when regulatory obligations are affected.

---

# Risk Acceptance Duration

Risk acceptance approvals should not be indefinite.

Recommended durations:

| Risk Severity | Maximum Duration |
|---|---|
| Low | 12 Months |
| Moderate | 6 Months |
| High | 3 Months |

Renewal should require reassessment and reapproval.

---

# Monitoring Requirements

Accepted risks should remain actively monitored.

Monitoring activities include:

- Vendor reassessments
- Compensating control reviews
- Security incident reviews
- Regulatory monitoring
- Governance reporting
- Vendor performance monitoring

Monitoring frequency should align with risk severity and vendor tier.

---

# Periodic Review Requirements

All accepted risks require periodic review.

| Risk Severity | Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Low | Annual |
| Moderate | Quarterly |
| High | Monthly |

Reviews should evaluate:

- Current risk exposure
- Vendor improvements
- Control effectiveness
- Business dependency changes
- Closure opportunities

---

# Renewal Process

When acceptance periods expire:

1. Reassess risk
2. Review vendor status
3. Validate compensating controls
4. Update risk assessment
5. Obtain new approval

Renewals should not occur automatically.

Repeated renewals require elevated governance scrutiny.

---

# Risk Acceptance Closure

Accepted risks should be closed when:

- Vendor remediation completed
- Risk eliminated
- Vendor relationship terminated
- Alternative controls implemented
- Vendor replaced

Closure records should include:

- Closure date
- Resolution summary
- Validation evidence
- Updated risk status

---

# Escalation Requirements

Accepted risks should be escalated when:

- Risk exposure increases
- Compensating controls fail
- Security incidents occur
- Regulatory obligations change
- Vendor risk exceeds tolerance
- Multiple renewals occur

Escalation should follow the Escalation Procedures Framework.

---

# Governance Reporting

Vendor risk acceptance reporting should provide visibility into:

- Active accepted risks
- Expiring risk acceptances
- High-risk accepted exposures
- Accepted risks by vendor tier
- Accepted risks by business unit
- Renewal activity
- Closure activity

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Recommended metrics include:

| KPI | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Active Risk Acceptances | Measures accepted exposure |
| High-Risk Acceptances | Measures executive exposure |
| Acceptance Renewal Rate | Measures unresolved risk trends |
| Expired Acceptances | Measures governance compliance |
| Acceptance Closure Rate | Measures remediation effectiveness |

---

# Audit Readiness Considerations

Documentation should demonstrate:

- Risk assessments
- Approval records
- Compensating controls
- Monitoring activities
- Review activities
- Renewal approvals
- Closure records

All records should be retained according to organizational retention requirements.

---

---

# Summary

The Vendor Risk Acceptance Process establishes a structured governance framework for formally accepting, monitoring, and reviewing vendor-related risks that cannot be fully remediated.

By requiring documented assessments, compensating controls, executive approvals, periodic reviews, and ongoing monitoring, the organization ensures accepted vendor risks remain visible, accountable, and aligned with enterprise risk tolerance.
