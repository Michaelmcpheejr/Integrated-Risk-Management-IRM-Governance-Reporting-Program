# Vendor Security Questionnaire Process

## Overview

The Vendor Security Questionnaire Process establishes the governance framework used to assess, evaluate, validate, and monitor the security posture of third-party vendors throughout the vendor lifecycle.

The purpose of this process is to:

- Evaluate vendor security controls
- Identify third-party risk exposure
- Support vendor onboarding decisions
- Validate vendor governance maturity
- Strengthen supply chain security
- Support regulatory compliance obligations
- Improve audit readiness
- Enable risk-based vendor management

Security questionnaires provide a standardized mechanism for evaluating a vendor's ability to protect organizational information, systems, and business processes.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Organizations inherit risk from third parties.

Before granting vendors access to:

- Sensitive data
- Business systems
- Internal networks
- Customer information
- Critical business processes

the organization should understand the vendor's security posture.

The objective is not to achieve perfect security.

The objective is to:

- Understand risk
- Identify control gaps
- Require remediation where appropriate
- Make informed business decisions

---

# Questionnaire Objectives

The Vendor Security Questionnaire Program seeks to:

- Assess vendor security maturity
- Validate security control implementation
- Identify governance weaknesses
- Evaluate regulatory compliance readiness
- Support vendor tiering decisions
- Improve third-party risk visibility
- Establish consistent assessment standards

---

# Assessment Triggers

Security questionnaires should be completed when:

- New vendor onboarding
- Contract renewal
- Service expansion
- Significant architecture changes
- Regulatory requirement changes
- Security incidents involving the vendor
- Executive review requests
- Vendor reassessment activities

---

# Questionnaire Lifecycle

All vendor assessments should follow a standardized workflow.

```text
Vendor Identification
          ↓
Vendor Tier Assignment
          ↓
Questionnaire Distribution
          ↓
Vendor Response Collection
          ↓
Evidence Review
          ↓
Assessment Scoring
          ↓
Risk Determination
          ↓
Remediation Review
          ↓
Approval Decision
          ↓
Vendor Monitoring
```

---

# Assessment Scope

Questionnaires should evaluate the following domains.

---

# Security Governance

Assessment Areas:

- Security program existence
- Security leadership
- Governance structure
- Security policies
- Risk management processes

Sample Questions:

- Do you maintain a documented information security program?
- Is security governance reviewed by leadership?
- Are security policies reviewed annually?

Evidence Examples:

- Security Policy
- Governance Charter
- Risk Management Policy

---

# Access Management

Assessment Areas:

- User provisioning
- User deprovisioning
- Role-based access control
- Privileged access management
- MFA implementation

Sample Questions:

- Is MFA enforced for privileged users?
- Are access reviews performed regularly?
- Is role-based access control implemented?

Evidence Examples:

- Access Control Policy
- Access Review Records
- MFA Configuration Evidence

---

# Asset Management

Assessment Areas:

- Asset inventory
- Asset ownership
- Asset lifecycle management

Sample Questions:

- Do you maintain an asset inventory?
- Are assets assigned ownership?

Evidence Examples:

- Asset Inventory
- Asset Management Policy

---

# Vulnerability Management

Assessment Areas:

- Vulnerability scanning
- Patch management
- Risk prioritization
- Remediation processes

Sample Questions:

- Are vulnerability scans performed regularly?
- How quickly are critical vulnerabilities remediated?

Evidence Examples:

- Vulnerability Reports
- Patch Management Procedures

---

# Security Monitoring

Assessment Areas:

- Logging
- Monitoring
- Detection capabilities
- Incident identification

Sample Questions:

- Are security events monitored continuously?
- Is centralized logging implemented?

Evidence Examples:

- SIEM Documentation
- Monitoring Procedures

---

# Incident Response

Assessment Areas:

- Incident handling
- Escalation procedures
- Communication plans
- Recovery procedures

Sample Questions:

Evidence Examples:

- Incident Response Plan

---

# Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

Assessment Areas:

- Recovery planning

Sample Questions:

- Do you maintain disaster recovery procedures?
- Are recovery exercises conducted?

Evidence Examples:

- Business Continuity Plan
- Disaster Recovery Plan

---

# Data Protection

Assessment Areas:

- Encryption
- Data classification
- Data retention
- Data destruction

Sample Questions:

- Is sensitive data encrypted at rest?
- Is sensitive data encrypted in transit?

Evidence Examples:

- Encryption Standards

# Regulatory Compliance

Assessment Areas:

- Regulatory obligations
- Compliance frameworks

Sample Questions:

- Have you experienced regulatory findings in the last 12 months?

Evidence Examples:

- SOC Reports
- ISO Certifications
- Compliance Attestations

---

# Evidence Validation Process

Questionnaire responses should be validated whenever possible.

Preferred evidence includes:

| Evidence Type | Reliability |
|---|---|
| Independent Audit Reports | High |
| Certifications | High |
| Technical Evidence | High |
| Internal Documentation | Moderate |
| Verbal Attestation | Low |

Vendor claims should not be accepted without supporting evidence whenever evidence is available.

---

# Assessment Scoring Methodology

Questionnaire responses should be evaluated using the organization's Risk Scoring Methodology.

Assessment dimensions:

- Likelihood
- Business Impact
- Regulatory Exposure
- Control Maturity

Results should be documented within the Vendor Risk Register.

---

# Assessment Outcomes

Following assessment, vendors should receive one of four outcomes.

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Approved | Risk acceptable |
| Conditionally Approved | Approved with required remediation |
| Risk Acceptance Required | Business accepts identified risk |
| Rejected | Risk exceeds tolerance |

All decisions should be documented.

---

# Remediation Management

When deficiencies are identified:

1. Create vendor finding
2. Assign remediation owner
3. Define remediation timeline
4. Track progress
5. Validate completion

Remediation activities should align with the Issue Management Framework.

---

# Reassessment Requirements

Questionnaire reassessment frequency should align with vendor tier.

| Vendor Tier | Assessment Frequency |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Annual |
| Tier 2 | Annual |
| Tier 3 | Every 2 Years |
| Tier 4 | As Needed |

Additional assessments may occur based on:

- Security incidents
- Service changes
- Regulatory changes
- Contract renewals

---

# Escalation Requirements

Assessment results should be escalated when:

- Significant security deficiencies identified
- Regulatory exposure exists
- Critical vendors fail assessment
- Remediation deadlines missed
- Executive approval required

Escalation should follow the Escalation Procedures Framework.

---

# Governance Reporting

Reporting should provide visibility into:

- Completed assessments
- Pending assessments
- Vendor findings
- Open remediation items
- High-risk vendors
- Assessment trends
- Risk acceptance decisions

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Recommended metrics include:

| KPI | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Assessment Completion Rate | Measures governance effectiveness |
| Open Vendor Findings | Measures remediation workload |
| High-Risk Vendors | Measures organizational exposure |
| Vendor Remediation Completion Rate | Measures risk reduction effectiveness |
| Assessment Aging | Measures assessment efficiency |

---

# Audit Readiness Considerations

The organization should retain:

- Completed questionnaires
- Assessment results
- Supporting evidence
- Vendor findings
- Remediation records
- Approval decisions
- Risk acceptance records

Documentation should support audits, regulatory reviews, and governance reporting.

# Summary

The Vendor Security Questionnaire Process establishes a structured and repeatable approach for evaluating vendor security posture, validating control effectiveness, identifying third-party risk exposure, and supporting informed onboarding and governance decisions.

By combining questionnaires, evidence validation, risk scoring, remediation tracking, and ongoing monitoring, the organization strengthens supply chain security, compliance readiness, and enterprise governance maturity.
