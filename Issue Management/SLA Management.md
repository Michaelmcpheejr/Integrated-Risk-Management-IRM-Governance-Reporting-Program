# SLA Management Framework

## Overview

The SLA Management Framework establishes standardized remediation timelines, escalation requirements, review frequencies, and governance accountability for risks, issues, audit findings, compliance deficiencies, and remediation activities throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of this framework is to:

- Define remediation expectations
- Improve accountability
- Reduce unresolved governance issues
- Strengthen escalation consistency
- Support executive visibility
- Improve audit readiness
- Align remediation efforts with organizational risk tolerance

Service Level Agreements (SLAs) establish governance expectations for how quickly identified risks and issues should be addressed.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Not all risks require the same response timeline.

The organization prioritizes remediation efforts based on:

- Risk Severity
- Business Impact
- Regulatory Exposure
- Asset Criticality
- Control Maturity
- Operational Dependency

The objective of SLA governance is to ensure remediation resources are focused on the areas creating the greatest organizational exposure.

---

# SLA Governance Principles

The SLA framework operates using the following principles:

| Principle | Description |
|------------|------------|
| Risk-Based Prioritization | Higher risks require faster remediation. |
| Accountability | Every remediation activity has an assigned owner. |
| Transparency | SLA status remains visible through governance reporting. |
| Escalation | Missed deadlines trigger defined escalation procedures. |
| Audit Defensibility | SLA decisions are documented and traceable. |
| Executive Visibility | Significant SLA failures receive leadership attention. |

---

# SLA Categories

SLAs are established based on Risk Severity.

## Low Risk

### Characteristics

- Limited business impact
- Minimal regulatory exposure
- Low operational dependency

### Remediation Timeline

- 6 to 12 Months

### Governance Oversight

- Management Review

### Escalation Trigger

- 30 Days Overdue

### Review Frequency

- Annual

---

## Moderate Risk

### Characteristics

- Meaningful business impact
- Moderate regulatory exposure
- Important business process dependency

### Remediation Timeline

- 30 to 60 Days

### Governance Oversight

- Director-Level Review

### Escalation Trigger

- 15 Days Overdue

### Review Frequency

- Quarterly

---

## High Risk

### Characteristics

- Severe business impact
- Significant regulatory exposure
- Critical business dependency

### Remediation Timeline

- 15 to 30 Days

### Governance Oversight

- Executive Leadership

### Escalation Trigger

- Immediate Escalation

### Review Frequency

- Monthly

---

# SLA Matrix

| Severity | Remediation Target | Escalation Trigger | Review Frequency |
|------------|------------|------------|------------|
| Low | 6-12 Months | 30 Days Overdue | Annual |
| Moderate | 30-60 Days | 15 Days Overdue | Quarterly |
| High | 15-30 Days | Immediate | Monthly |

---

# Asset Criticality Integration

Asset criticality may accelerate remediation requirements.

| Asset Criticality | SLA Adjustment |
|------------|------------|
| Low | No Adjustment |
| Moderate | Increase Review Priority |
| High | Accelerated Escalation & Executive Visibility |

Example:

A Moderate Risk affecting a High Criticality asset may receive accelerated remediation requirements based on business impact.

---

# Regulatory Exposure Integration

Regulatory obligations may override standard SLA requirements.

Examples:

- HIPAA breach notification requirements
- Regulatory reporting deadlines
- Customer contractual obligations
- Audit remediation commitments

Where regulatory deadlines exist, the shorter deadline takes precedence.

---

# Remediation Extension Process

In certain circumstances remediation timelines may require extension.

Acceptable reasons may include:

- Resource constraints
- Technology limitations
- Vendor dependencies
- Operational requirements
- Planned replacement initiatives

Extensions require:

- Business justification
- Updated remediation plan
- Executive approval for High Risks
- Revised completion date

All approved extensions should be documented.

---

# SLA Exception Governance

SLA exceptions should be limited and formally approved.

Required documentation:

- Issue ID
- Risk ID
- Original Due Date
- Requested Extension
- Business Justification
- Risk Impact Assessment
- Approval Authority

Repeated SLA exceptions should trigger governance review.

---

# Overdue Remediation Governance

Overdue items require immediate visibility.

Governance reporting should identify:

- Open overdue items
- Severity level
- Days overdue
- Responsible owner
- Escalation status
- Business impact

Repeated overdue items should be escalated through the Escalation Procedures Framework.

---

# Executive Escalation Requirements

The following automatically require executive visibility:

- High Risks overdue beyond target date
- Regulatory findings overdue beyond target date
- Material audit findings
- Critical vendor remediation delays
- Risks affecting High Criticality assets
- Repeated SLA violations

Executive review should determine:

- Additional resources
- Alternative remediation strategies
- Risk acceptance consideration
- Escalation to governance committees

---

# Governance Reporting Metrics

The SLA program should track:

| Metric | Description |
|------------|------------|
| Open Remediation Items | Total active remediation efforts |
| Overdue Items | Items exceeding SLA |
| High-Risk Overdue Items | Critical governance concern |
| Average Days to Remediate | Operational effectiveness metric |
| SLA Compliance Rate | Percentage completed within SLA |
| Extension Requests | Measures remediation challenges |
| Repeat Offenders | Owners with repeated SLA violations |

# Governance Review Process

SLA performance should be reviewed:

| Audience | Frequency |
|------------|------------|
| Risk Owners | Monthly |
| Department Leadership | Quarterly |
| Executive Leadership | Quarterly |
| Governance Committee | Quarterly |

Reviews should evaluate:

- SLA performance trends
- Overdue remediation
- Escalation effectiveness
- Resource constraints
- Risk reduction progress

# Summary

The SLA Management Framework establishes consistent remediation expectations, escalation requirements, and governance accountability across the organization.

By aligning remediation timelines to risk severity, business impact, regulatory exposure, and asset criticality, the organization improves risk reduction, governance visibility, audit readiness, and executive oversight throughout the Integrated Risk Management program.
