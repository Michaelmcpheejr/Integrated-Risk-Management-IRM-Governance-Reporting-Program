# Issue Management Workflow

## Overview

The Issue Management Workflow defines the governance process used to identify, document, prioritize, remediate, validate, and close issues throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of this workflow is to:

- Establish a standardized issue management process
- Improve accountability for remediation activities
- Strengthen governance oversight
- Support audit readiness
- Improve risk visibility
- Ensure timely resolution of control deficiencies
- Provide executive visibility into unresolved issues

Issue Management serves as the operational bridge between identified risks and remediation activities.

---

# Governance Philosophy

An issue represents a condition requiring corrective action.

Issues may originate from:

- Risk Assessments
- Internal Audits
- External Audits
- Compliance Reviews
- Security Assessments
- Vulnerability Assessments
- Control Testing
- Policy Violations
- Third-Party Assessments
- Governance Reviews

The objective of Issue Management is not simply to document problems.

The objective is to:

- Assign ownership
- Drive remediation
- Validate resolution
- Reduce organizational risk

# Issue Lifecycle

All issues follow a standardized lifecycle.

```text
Issue Identification
          ↓
Issue Assessment
          ↓
Issue Classification
          ↓
Ownership Assignment
          ↓
Remediation Planning
          ↓
Remediation Execution

          ↓
