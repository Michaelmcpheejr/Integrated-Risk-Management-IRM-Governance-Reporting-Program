# Remediation Tracking Framework

## Overview

The Remediation Tracking Framework establishes the governance process used to monitor, manage, validate, and report corrective actions resulting from identified risks, issues, audit findings, compliance gaps, security deficiencies, and third-party assessments.

The purpose of this framework is to:

- Ensure identified issues receive corrective action
- Improve accountability for remediation activities
- Increase visibility into remediation progress
- Support governance oversight and escalation
- Improve audit readiness
- Reduce recurring control failures
- Strengthen enterprise risk management operations

Remediation tracking ensures that governance activities result in measurable risk reduction rather than documentation alone.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Identifying issues is only the first step.

The true measure of governance effectiveness is whether issues are:

- Assigned
- Remediated
- Validated
- Reported
- Closed

The organization views remediation tracking as a core governance function that provides visibility into risk reduction efforts across the enterprise.

---

# Remediation Lifecycle

All remediation activities follow a standardized lifecycle.

```text
Issue Identification
          ↓
Ownership Assignment
          ↓
Remediation Planning
          ↓
Remediation Approval
          ↓
Implementation
          ↓
Progress Monitoring
          ↓
Validation Review
          ↓
Closure Approval
          ↓
Closed
```

---

# Remediation Objectives

Every remediation effort should achieve one or more of the following objectives:

- Reduce risk exposure
- Improve control effectiveness
- Address compliance deficiencies
- Strengthen governance processes
- Eliminate audit findings
- Improve operational resilience
- Reduce regulatory exposure

---

# Remediation Ownership Structure

Every remediation activity must have assigned ownership.

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Remediation Owner | Responsible for implementation |
| Reviewer | Validates remediation effectiveness |
| Executive Sponsor | Provides oversight for significant remediation efforts |
| GRC | Tracks progress and governance reporting |

Remediation activities without assigned ownership should not be considered active.

---

# Remediation Status Model

All remediation activities should follow a standardized status structure.

| Status | Description |
|---|---|
| Not Started | Remediation has not begun |
| Planned | Remediation plan approved |
| In Progress | Remediation activities underway |
| On Hold | Progress temporarily suspended |
| Pending Validation | Remediation completed awaiting review |
| Closed | Validation completed and approved |
| Risk Accepted | Remediation not pursued; risk formally accepted |

---

# Remediation Priority Model

Remediation priority is driven by risk severity.

| Risk Severity | Priority |
|---|---|
| Low | Standard |
| Moderate | Elevated |
| High | Critical |

Priority influences:

- Resource allocation
- Escalation requirements
- Reporting frequency
- Target completion dates

---

# Required Remediation Documentation

Every remediation activity should include:

- Remediation ID
- Related Issue ID
- Related Risk ID
- Remediation Description
- Assigned Owner
- Due Date
- Current Status
- Validation Method
- Supporting Evidence
- Closure Date

---

# Remediation Planning Requirements

Every remediation effort should include a documented plan.

The plan should identify:

- Corrective actions
- Required resources
- Dependencies
- Expected outcomes
- Target completion date
- Validation approach

Example:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Issue | Excessive Privileged Access |
| Remediation | Implement quarterly access review process |
| Owner | IAM Manager |
| Due Date | 30 Days |
| Validation | Review completed access review records |

---

# Progress Monitoring

Remediation progress should be monitored throughout implementation.

Monitoring activities include:

- Status updates
- Milestone reviews
- Dependency reviews
- Resource assessments
- Escalation reviews

Progress reporting should identify:

- Completed actions
- Remaining work
- Delays
- Blockers
- Risk increases

---

# Validation Requirements

Completion of work does not automatically close a remediation activity.

Validation is required to confirm:

- Corrective actions were implemented
- Controls are operating effectively
- Supporting evidence exists
- Risk exposure has been reduced

Validation methods may include:

- Control testing
- Configuration review
- Evidence review
- Security validation
- Audit validation
- Compliance review

---

# Validation Outcomes

Validation results should be classified as:

| Result | Description |
|---|---|
| Successful | Remediation verified and effective |
| Partially Successful | Improvements made but gaps remain |
| Unsuccessful | Remediation failed or insufficient |

Partially successful and unsuccessful validations require additional remediation planning.

---

# Closure Requirements

Before remediation can be closed:

- Implementation completed
- Validation performed
- Evidence documented
- Reviewer approval obtained
- Governance records updated

Closure records should include:

- Validation evidence
- Closure approval
- Updated risk status
- Residual risk determination

---

# Overdue Remediation Governance

Remediation activities exceeding target dates require escalation.

| Severity | Escalation Trigger |
|---|---|
| Low | 30 Days Overdue |
| Moderate | 15 Days Overdue |
| High | Immediate Escalation |

Escalation may include:

- Management notification
- Director review
- Executive review
- Governance committee reporting

---

# Residual Risk Integration

Some remediation efforts may not fully eliminate risk.

When residual risk remains:

1. Reassess risk exposure
2. Evaluate compensating controls
3. Determine residual risk score
4. Initiate risk acceptance process if necessary

Residual risks should be tracked through the Residual Risk Framework.

---

# Governance Reporting

Remediation reporting should provide visibility into:

- Open remediation activities
- Overdue remediation items
- High-risk remediation efforts
- Remediation completion rates
- Validation outcomes
- Residual risk trends
- Business unit performance

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Recommended remediation metrics include:

| KPI | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Open Remediation Count | Measures remediation workload |
| Overdue Remediation Count | Measures governance effectiveness |
| Average Time to Remediate | Measures operational efficiency |
| Validation Success Rate | Measures remediation quality |
| High-Risk Remediation Completion Rate | Measures risk reduction effectiveness |

---

# Governance Review Frequency

| Severity | Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Low | Quarterly |
| Moderate | Monthly |
| High | Weekly |

---

# Audit Readiness Considerations

The remediation process should demonstrate:

- Ownership accountability
- Progress tracking
- Validation evidence
- Escalation activities
- Closure approvals
- Risk reduction outcomes

All remediation records should be retained for audit and regulatory review.

# Summary

The Remediation Tracking Framework establishes a structured and repeatable process for driving issues through corrective action, validation, and closure.

By enforcing accountability, monitoring progress, validating effectiveness, and reporting outcomes, the organization ensures remediation activities contribute to measurable risk reduction and governance maturity.
