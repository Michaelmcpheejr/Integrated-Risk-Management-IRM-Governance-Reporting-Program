# Escalation Procedures Framework

## Overview

The Escalation Procedures Framework defines the governance process used to elevate risks, issues, remediation delays, control failures, compliance concerns, and third-party findings to appropriate levels of management and executive leadership.

The purpose of this framework is to:

- Ensure significant issues receive appropriate attention
- Improve governance accountability
- Prevent unresolved issues from remaining hidden
- Strengthen executive visibility
- Support timely decision-making
- Improve audit readiness
- Align remediation efforts with organizational risk tolerance

Escalation procedures provide a structured path for raising concerns when risks exceed acceptable thresholds or governance expectations.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Not every issue requires executive involvement.

However, issues that create significant business, regulatory, operational, financial, or security exposure must receive elevated governance attention.

Escalation exists to ensure:

- Risks remain visible
- Decisions occur at the appropriate authority level
- Accountability is maintained
- Governance obligations are fulfilled
- Remediation efforts remain aligned with organizational priorities

Escalation should be viewed as a governance safeguard rather than a failure.

---

# Escalation Triggers

Escalation should occur when one or more of the following conditions exist:

- High Risk identified
- Critical business asset affected
- Regulatory exposure exists
- Remediation becomes overdue
- Control failure remains unresolved
- Risk acceptance is requested
- Audit finding remains open
- Executive decision required
- Significant business disruption occurs
- Third-party risk exceeds organizational tolerance

---

# Escalation Levels

The organization utilizes a four-level escalation structure.

---

## Level 1: Operational Escalation

### Audience

- Risk Owner
- Issue Owner
- Team Manager

### Typical Triggers

- Newly identified issue
- Low Risk findings
- Minor remediation delays
- Standard governance concerns

### Objective

Resolve issue through normal operational management processes.

### Review Frequency

As needed during normal operations.

---

## Level 2: Management Escalation

### Audience

- Department Manager
- Business Owner
- Control Owner

### Typical Triggers

- Moderate Risk findings
- Repeated control failures
- Moderate remediation delays
- Recurring governance issues

### Objective

Increase visibility and ensure remediation resources are allocated appropriately.

### Review Frequency

Monthly

---

## Level 3: Leadership Escalation

### Audience

- Director
- Department Head
- Senior Leadership

### Typical Triggers

- High Risk findings
- Significant audit findings
- Compliance deficiencies
- High-value asset exposure
- Repeated missed remediation deadlines

### Objective

Drive leadership intervention and decision-making.

### Review Frequency

Monthly or as required.

---

## Level 4: Executive Escalation

### Audience

- CISO
- CIO
- CRO
- CFO
- Executive Risk Committee
- Governance Committee

### Typical Triggers

- Severe Regulatory Exposure
- Executive Risk Acceptance Requests
- Material Control Failures
- Major Audit Findings
- Enterprise-Wide Risk Exposure
- Significant Third-Party Risk
- Board-Level Risk Concerns

### Objective

Support executive decision-making and enterprise governance oversight.

### Review Frequency

Monthly governance review or emergency escalation.

---

# Escalation Matrix

| Condition | Escalation Level |
|------------|------------|
| Low Risk | Level 1 |
| Moderate Risk | Level 2 |
| High Risk | Level 3 |
| High Risk + Regulatory Exposure | Level 4 |
| Risk Acceptance Request | Level 4 |
| High Criticality Asset Exposure | Level 4 |
| Overdue High Risk Remediation | Level 4 |
| Material Audit Finding | Level 4 |
| Vendor Risk Above Tolerance | Level 4 |

---

# Remediation Escalation Triggers

Escalation requirements increase when remediation deadlines are missed.

| Severity | Escalation Trigger |
|------------|------------|
| Low | 30 Days Overdue |
| Moderate | 15 Days Overdue |
| High | Immediate Escalation |

Escalation actions may include:

- Management notification
- Leadership review
- Executive reporting
- Governance committee review

---

# Risk Acceptance Escalation

All formal risk acceptance requests require escalation.

Required documentation:

- Risk Description
- Current Risk Score
- Residual Risk Score
- Business Justification
- Compensating Controls
- Risk Owner Recommendation

Approval Authority:

| Risk Severity | Approval Authority |
|------------|------------|
| Low | Department Manager |
| Moderate | Director |
| High | Executive Leadership |
| Regulatory Exposure | Executive Leadership + Compliance Review |

---

# Audit Finding Escalation

Audit findings require escalation based on severity.

| Audit Finding Severity | Escalation Level |
|------------|------------|
| Observation | Level 1 |
| Moderate Finding | Level 2 |
| Significant Finding | Level 3 |
| Material Finding | Level 4 |

Repeated audit findings automatically escalate one level higher.

---

# Compliance Escalation

Compliance issues require escalation when:

- Regulatory obligations are violated
- Control requirements are unmet
- Audit commitments are missed
- Regulatory deadlines approach
- Breach notification requirements exist

Compliance escalation should include:

- Compliance Team
- Legal Team
- Executive Leadership (when required)

---

# Third-Party Risk Escalation

Third-party findings should escalate when:

- Vendor risk exceeds tolerance
- Critical vendor lacks required controls
- Remediation commitments are missed
- Regulatory obligations are affected

Examples:

- Vendor stores regulated data
- Vendor fails security assessment
- Vendor refuses remediation

High-risk vendor concerns require executive visibility.

---

# Governance Reporting Integration

Escalated items should appear within governance reporting.

Reporting should include:

- Escalated risks
- Escalated issues
- Overdue remediation
- Risk acceptance requests
- Audit findings
- Compliance concerns
- Third-party risk concerns

---

# Escalation Documentation Requirements

Every escalation should include:

- Escalation Date
- Escalation Level
- Reason for Escalation
- Related Risk ID
- Related Issue ID
- Assigned Stakeholders
- Required Actions
- Resolution Status

---

# Governance Review Requirements

Escalated items should remain under review until:

- Remediation completed
- Risk accepted
- Control validated
- Issue closed
- Executive decision documented

Escalation should never be removed solely because time has passed.

---

# Audit Readiness Considerations

The organization should maintain evidence demonstrating:

- Escalation criteria
- Escalation decisions
- Executive involvement
- Resolution activities
- Governance oversight

This documentation supports internal audits, external audits, regulatory reviews, and executive governance reporting.

# Summary

The Escalation Procedures Framework establishes a structured approach for elevating risks, issues, remediation delays, audit findings, compliance concerns, and third-party risks to appropriate levels of governance.

By defining escalation triggers, authority levels, review requirements, and documentation standards, the organization strengthens accountability, executive visibility, and enterprise risk oversight.
