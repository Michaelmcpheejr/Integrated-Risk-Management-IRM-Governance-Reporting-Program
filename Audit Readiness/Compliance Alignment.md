# Compliance Alignment Framework

## Overview

The Compliance Alignment Framework establishes how the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program aligns governance activities, risk management processes, control frameworks, audit readiness activities, and reporting structures with applicable regulatory, contractual, and industry requirements.

The purpose of compliance alignment is to:

- Support regulatory compliance obligations
- Improve governance consistency
- Strengthen audit readiness
- Demonstrate control coverage
- Reduce compliance gaps
- Improve executive visibility
- Support risk-based compliance management
- Enable framework mapping and traceability

The framework ensures governance activities support both business objectives and compliance requirements.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Compliance should not operate independently from risk management.

Traditional compliance programs often focus on:

- Passing audits
- Completing assessments
- Satisfying regulatory requirements

The IRM Governance Program focuses on:

- Understanding risk
- Managing risk
- Demonstrating compliance through governance
- Supporting business objectives

Compliance becomes a byproduct of effective governance.

---

# Compliance Alignment Objectives

The Compliance Alignment Program seeks to:

- Map governance activities to compliance requirements
- Reduce duplicated compliance efforts
- Improve framework traceability
- Support audit readiness
- Improve reporting consistency
- Centralize governance operations
- Improve regulatory defensibility

---

# Supported Frameworks

The program is designed to support multiple frameworks simultaneously.

Primary frameworks include:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- NIST SP 800-53
- ISO/IEC 27001
- HIPAA Security Rule
- SOX IT General Controls (ITGC)

The governance model is framework-agnostic and can support additional regulatory requirements when necessary.

---

# Governance-to-Compliance Mapping Model

The organization utilizes a centralized compliance mapping structure.

```text
Framework Requirement
          ↓
Policy
          ↓
Control
          ↓
Risk
          ↓
Assessment
          ↓
Evidence
          ↓
Reporting
```

This structure improves consistency and reduces duplicated compliance efforts.

# Summary

The Compliance Alignment Framework establishes a unified governance approach for supporting regulatory, contractual, and industry requirements through centralized risk management, control governance, audit readiness, evidence management, and executive reporting.

By aligning governance activities to recognized frameworks, the organization improves compliance posture, audit defensibility, governance maturity, and enterprise risk management effectiveness.
