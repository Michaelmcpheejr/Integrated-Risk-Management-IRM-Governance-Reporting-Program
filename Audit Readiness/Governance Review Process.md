# Governance Review Process

## Overview

The Governance Review Process establishes the framework used to evaluate, monitor, review, and continuously improve governance activities throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of governance reviews is to:

- Evaluate program effectiveness
- Assess governance maturity
- Identify process deficiencies
- Validate risk management activities
- Support executive oversight
- Improve audit readiness
- Drive continuous improvement
- Maintain alignment with organizational objectives

Governance reviews ensure that governance processes remain effective, efficient, and aligned with business, regulatory, and risk management requirements.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Governance is not a one-time activity.

Effective governance programs require ongoing evaluation to ensure:

- Risks are properly managed
- Controls remain effective
- Issues are remediated
- Compliance obligations are satisfied
- Governance objectives are achieved

The objective of governance reviews is not to create additional oversight.

The objective is to continuously improve governance effectiveness.

---

# Governance Review Objectives

The Governance Review Program seeks to:

- Validate governance effectiveness
- Improve risk visibility
- Monitor control performance
- Evaluate remediation effectiveness
- Identify governance gaps
- Support executive decision making
- Improve program maturity

---

All governance reviews should follow a structured lifecycle.

```text

        ↓
Data Collection
        ↓
Governance Evaluation
        ↓
Gap Identification
        ↓
Recommendations
        ↓
Leadership Review
        ↓
Action Planning
        ↓

```

---

# Governance Review Structure

The organization utilizes a layered review model.

```text
Operational Reviews
        ↓
Management Reviews
        ↓
Executive Reviews
        ↓
Governance Committee Reviews
        ↓
Annual Program Review
```

Each layer provides progressively broader governance oversight.

| Domain | Assessment Focus |
|----------|----------|
| Risk Management | Identification, assessment, treatment |
| Control Governance | Design and effectiveness |
| Issue Management | Remediation and accountability |
| Vendor Risk | Third-party oversight |
| Reporting | Executive visibility |
| Audit Readiness | Evidence and traceability |

# Escalation Criteria

Governance reviews should trigger escalation when:

- High-risk issues remain unresolved
- Regulatory exposure increases
- Critical vendors experience failures
- SLA performance declines
- Governance metrics exceed thresholds
- Control effectiveness deteriorates

Escalations should follow the Escalation Procedures Framework.

# Summary

The Governance Review Process establishes a structured approach for evaluating governance effectiveness, monitoring program performance, identifying improvement opportunities, and supporting executive oversight.

By implementing layered governance reviews, the organization strengthens risk management, improves compliance readiness, enhances audit defensibility, and drives continuous governance maturity across the enterprise.
