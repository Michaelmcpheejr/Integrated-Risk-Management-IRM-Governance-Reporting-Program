# Risk Ownership Model

## Overview

The Risk Ownership Model establishes accountability, oversight, review, approval, and governance responsibilities throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of this model is to:

- Establish clear accountability for risk management activities
- Define governance responsibilities across stakeholders
- Support enterprise risk decision-making
- Improve remediation ownership and follow-through
- Strengthen executive oversight
- Improve audit defensibility
- Ensure governance decisions are properly documented and approved

This model follows a hybrid governance structure where risk management is coordinated by GRC, validated by subject matter experts, and approved through leadership governance processes.

---

# Governance Philosophy

The organization operates under a shared accountability model.

Risk management is not solely the responsibility of GRC, Security, Compliance, Legal, or Leadership.

Instead:

- GRC facilitates governance
- Business owners own risk
- Security validates technical exposure
- Compliance validates regulatory exposure
- Leadership approves risk decisions

This structure improves accountability while maintaining independent governance oversight.
