# Asset Criticality Model

## Overview

The Asset Criticality Model establishes a standardized methodology for identifying, classifying, prioritizing, and governing business and technology assets throughout the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of this model is to:

- Identify assets most critical to organizational operations
- Support risk prioritization and governance decision-making
- Improve executive visibility into business-critical assets
- Strengthen control allocation and remediation prioritization
- Support audit readiness and regulatory compliance
- Improve traceability between assets, risks, controls, and issues
- Align governance reporting with enterprise risk management objectives

Asset criticality serves as a foundational component of enterprise risk governance by ensuring resources are focused on assets that create the greatest business impact if compromised, disrupted, or mismanaged.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Not all assets carry equal business value.

Some assets support mission-critical operations, regulatory obligations, customer services, or revenue generation, while others have limited organizational impact.

The Asset Criticality Model ensures:

- Governance efforts focus on the most important assets
- Risk assessments remain aligned to business priorities
- Executive reporting reflects actual organizational exposure
- Control implementation is prioritized appropriately
- Third-party risk decisions consider asset importance

Asset criticality should directly influence:

- Risk prioritization
- Remediation timelines
- Governance escalation
- Review frequency
- Executive reporting visibility

# Asset Categories

Assets are classified into two primary categories.

## Business Assets

Business Assets directly support organizational operations and business objectives.

Examples:

- Payroll Process
- Revenue Management Process
- Financial Reporting Process
- Customer Service Operations
- Human Resources Operations
- Vendor Management Program
- Compliance Program

---

## Technology Assets

Technology Assets support business operations through systems, applications, infrastructure, or services.

Examples:

- Active Directory
- ERP Systems
- CRM Platforms
- Financial Databases
- Cloud Infrastructure
- Network Infrastructure
- Identity Management Systems
- Security Monitoring Platforms

# Asset Criticality Scoring Model

Assets are evaluated using a simplified 3-point classification model.

This model improves consistency and governance defensibility while reducing classification ambiguity.

| Score | Criticality Level |
|---------|---------|
| 1 | Low |
| 2 | Moderate |
| 3 | High |

# Criticality Assessment Factors

Asset criticality should be determined using the following factors:

### Business Dependency

- How dependent is the organization on the asset?
- Can operations continue without it?

### Financial Impact

- What financial losses occur if the asset becomes unavailable?

### Regulatory Impact

- Does the asset support regulated data or compliance obligations?

### Operational Impact

- How many departments or business processes depend on the asset?

### Customer Impact

- Would customers be affected by disruption?

### Recovery Complexity

- How difficult is recovery if the asset becomes unavailable?

### Data Sensitivity

- Does the asset store confidential, regulated, or critical business information?

# Criticality Classification

## Level 1: Low Criticality

### Definition

The asset supports limited business functions and its disruption would have minimal organizational impact.

### Characteristics

- Limited operational dependency
- Minimal financial impact
- No significant regulatory implications
- Small user population affected
- Recovery is straightforward

### Examples

- Training Platforms
- Internal Knowledge Bases
- Non-Critical Department Applications
- Test Environments

### Governance Requirements

- Annual review
- Standard remediation timelines
- Limited executive visibility

---

## Level 2: Moderate Criticality

### Definition

The asset supports important business functions and disruption would cause noticeable operational impact.

### Characteristics

- Multiple teams rely on the asset
- Moderate financial consequences
- Potential audit implications
- Recovery requires coordination

### Examples

- Human Resources Systems
- Procurement Platforms
- Departmental Databases
- Internal Reporting Systems

### Governance Requirements

- Quarterly review
- Prioritized remediation
- Director-level visibility

---

## Level 3: High Criticality

### Definition

The asset supports mission-critical business operations, regulatory obligations, revenue generation, or enterprise services.

### Characteristics

- Significant operational dependency
- Major financial impact
- Regulatory implications
- Enterprise-wide impact
- Difficult recovery
- Executive visibility required

### Examples

- Active Directory
- ERP Systems
- Financial Reporting Systems
- Payroll Systems
- Customer Databases
- Cloud Infrastructure
- Security Monitoring Platforms
- Identity & Access Management Systems

### Governance Requirements

- Monthly review
- Accelerated remediation requirements
- Executive visibility
- Governance committee reporting

# Asset Ownership Structure

Every asset should have assigned governance ownership.

| Role | Responsibility |
|---------|---------|
| Asset Owner | Accountable for asset governance and risk decisions |
| Technical Owner | Responsible for operational management |
| Reviewer | Validates asset classification and criticality |
| Executive Sponsor | Provides leadership oversight for High Criticality assets |
