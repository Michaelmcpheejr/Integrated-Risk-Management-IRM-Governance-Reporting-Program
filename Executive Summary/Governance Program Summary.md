# Governance Program Summary

## Executive Summary

The Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program was designed to establish a centralized governance framework capable of managing enterprise risks, compliance obligations, issue remediation activities, audit readiness initiatives, executive reporting, and third-party risk oversight through a structured and repeatable operating model.

The program aligns governance activities to enterprise Integrated Risk Management (IRM) concepts commonly found within platforms such as ServiceNow IRM, RSA Archer, MetricStream, and AuditBoard while remaining framework-agnostic and adaptable to multiple regulatory environments.

The primary objective of the program is to improve organizational visibility into risk, strengthen governance accountability, support audit readiness, and enable leadership to make informed risk-based decisions through centralized reporting and governance workflows.

---

# Program Objectives

The program was established to achieve the following objectives:

- Centralize enterprise risk governance
- Standardize risk identification and assessment processes
- Improve risk ownership and accountability
- Formalize issue management and remediation tracking
- Enhance executive reporting capabilities
- Improve third-party risk oversight
- Strengthen audit readiness
- Improve governance traceability
- Align governance activities with regulatory and industry frameworks
- Support continuous governance improvement

The program emphasizes governance consistency, repeatability, and transparency across all business and technology domains.

---

# Governance Operating Model

The governance operating model establishes structured relationships between:

```text
Business Assets
        ↓
Risks
        ↓
Controls
        ↓
Assessments
        ↓
Issues
        ↓
Remediation Activities
        ↓
Validation
        ↓
Evidence
        ↓
Reporting
        ↓
Executive Oversight
```

This model improves governance visibility while enabling traceability across the entire risk lifecycle.

# Core Governance Domains

The program consists of six primary governance domains.

## Enterprise Risk Management

Supports:

- Risk Identification
- Risk Assessment
- Risk Scoring
- Risk Treatment
- Residual Risk Governance
- Risk Monitoring

Enterprise risks are managed through a centralized risk register and governed through formal ownership and escalation procedures.

---

## Issue Management & Remediation

Supports:

- Audit Findings
- Compliance Gaps
- Control Deficiencies
- Vendor Findings
- Policy Exceptions

Issues follow a structured lifecycle from identification through remediation, validation, and closure.

---

## Third-Party Risk Management

Supports:

- Vendor Tiering
- Inherent Risk Assessments
- Security Questionnaires
- Vendor Governance
- Continuous Monitoring
- Vendor Risk Acceptance

Third-party risks are managed through formal governance processes that provide visibility into supply chain and vendor-related exposures.

---

## Governance Reporting

Supports:

- Executive Dashboards
- Governance KPIs
- Leadership Reporting
- Risk Reporting
- Compliance Reporting

Reporting activities provide leadership with visibility into organizational risk posture, governance effectiveness, and remediation performance.

---

## Audit Readiness

Supports:

- Audit Evidence Collection
- Audit Traceability
- Compliance Alignment
- Governance Reviews
- Documentation Retention

Audit readiness processes improve governance defensibility and support regulatory and assessment activities.

---

## Compliance Alignment

Supports alignment with:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- NIST SP 800-53
- ISO/IEC 27001
- HIPAA Security Rule
- SOX IT General Controls (ITGC)

Compliance activities are integrated directly into governance operations rather than managed as standalone initiatives.

---

# Governance Philosophy

The program operates under four core governance principles:

## Risk-Based Decision Making

Governance activities should prioritize organizational risk exposure rather than checklist compliance.

---

## Accountability

Every risk, issue, remediation activity, and governance decision should have clearly assigned ownership.

---

## Traceability

Risks, controls, findings, remediation activities, and evidence should maintain documented relationships that support audit readiness and governance visibility.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Governance processes should be reviewed regularly and improved based on performance metrics, assessments, audits, and organizational changes.

---

# Executive Reporting Model

The program utilizes a layered reporting structure designed to provide appropriate governance visibility at each level of the organization.

```text
Operational Teams
        ↓
Management
        ↓
Directors
        ↓
Executive Leadership
        ↓
Governance Committee
        ↓
Board Oversight
```

This structure ensures governance information is delivered to stakeholders according to their responsibilities and decision-making authority.
