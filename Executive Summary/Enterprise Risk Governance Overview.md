# Enterprise Risk Governance Overview

## Executive Summary

The Enterprise Risk Governance Framework establishes the structure, accountability, decision-making processes, and oversight mechanisms used to identify, assess, manage, monitor, and report risks throughout the organization.

The purpose of enterprise risk governance is to ensure that risks are managed consistently, transparently, and in alignment with organizational objectives, regulatory obligations, and risk tolerance levels.

The framework provides leadership with visibility into enterprise risk exposure while establishing clear accountability for risk ownership, remediation activities, and governance decisions.

---

# Risk Governance Philosophy

The organization recognizes that risk cannot be eliminated.

Risk governance exists to ensure risks are:

- Identified
- Assessed
- Prioritized
- Managed
- Monitored
- Reported
- Reviewed

The objective is not to remove all risk.

The objective is to make informed risk-based decisions that support business objectives while maintaining acceptable levels of organizational exposure.

Effective governance enables leadership to understand:

- What risks exist
- How significant those risks are
- Who is responsible for managing them
- What actions are being taken
- What risks require executive attention

---

# Enterprise Risk Governance Objectives

The Enterprise Risk Governance Program seeks to:

- Improve enterprise risk visibility
- Standardize risk management practices
- Strengthen accountability
- Improve executive oversight
- Support regulatory compliance
- Improve audit readiness
- Enable informed decision-making
- Improve organizational resilience

# Governance Structure

Enterprise risk governance operates through a layered oversight model.

```text
Operational Teams
        ↓
Risk Owners
        ↓
Management
        ↓
Directors
        ↓
Executive Leadership
        ↓
Governance Committee
        ↓
Board Oversight
```

Each layer has defined governance responsibilities.

---

# Governance Roles & Responsibilities

## Risk Owners

Risk Owners are responsible for:

- Managing assigned risks
- Maintaining risk documentation
- Supporting risk assessments
- Coordinating remediation activities
- Reporting risk status

Risk ownership should be assigned to individuals with authority to influence risk outcomes.

---

## Control Owners

Control Owners are responsible for:

- Operating controls
- Maintaining evidence
- Supporting assessments
- Participating in validation activities

Control Owners support risk mitigation efforts through effective control operation.

---

## GRC Team

The Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) team is responsible for:

- Governance oversight
- Risk methodology administration
- Risk reporting
- Governance reviews
- Escalation management
- Audit readiness support

The GRC team serves as the central governance function.

---

## Executive Leadership

Executive Leadership is responsible for:

- Reviewing enterprise risk posture
- Approving risk acceptance decisions
- Allocating resources
- Supporting remediation efforts
- Setting governance priorities

Leadership accountability is critical to governance effectiveness.

---

## Governance Committee

The Governance Committee is responsible for:

- Oversight of enterprise risk activities
- Reviewing high-risk exposures
- Monitoring governance effectiveness
- Supporting strategic governance decisions

The committee provides independent oversight of governance operations.

---

## Board Oversight

The Board is responsible for:

- Strategic risk oversight
- Governance accountability
- Review of material risks
- Oversight of organizational resilience

Board involvement focuses on enterprise-level risk exposure rather than operational details.

---

```text

        ↓

        ↓
Risk Treatment
        ↓
Remediation
```

The lifecycle ensures risks remain visible throughout their existence.

# Risk Appetite & Risk Tolerance

Risk governance decisions should align with organizational risk tolerance levels.

The organization should define:

- Acceptable Risk
- Elevated Risk
- High Risk
- Unacceptable Risk

Risk tolerance thresholds support consistent governance decisions.

---

# Residual Risk Governance

Residual risk represents the risk that remains after controls and remediation activities have been implemented.

Residual risks should be:

- Evaluated
- Documented
- Reviewed
- Approved when necessary

Residual risk acceptance should occur only through formal governance processes.

---

# Risk Acceptance Governance

Risk acceptance may occur when:

- Remediation is not feasible
- Costs exceed expected benefit
- Compensating controls exist
- Business requirements justify acceptance

All risk acceptance decisions should include:

- Business justification
- Risk assessment results
- Approval records
- Review dates

Risk acceptance should never occur informally.

---

The Enterprise Risk Governance Framework establishes a centralized approach for managing organizational risk through structured governance processes, defined accountability, executive oversight, and continuous monitoring.

By integrating risk management, remediation governance, reporting, audit readiness, and compliance alignment into a unified governance model, the organization improves visibility, accountability, resilience, and long-term governance effectiveness while supporting informed risk-based decision-making across the enterprise.
