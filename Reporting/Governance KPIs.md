# Governance KPIs & Metrics Framework

## Overview

The Governance KPIs & Metrics Framework establishes the key performance indicators (KPIs), key risk indicators (KRIs), and governance metrics used to measure the effectiveness of the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of governance metrics is to:

- Provide executive visibility into organizational risk posture
- Measure governance program effectiveness
- Support risk-based decision making
- Improve accountability
- Track remediation progress
- Identify emerging trends
- Support audit readiness
- Drive continuous improvement

Metrics should provide actionable information rather than simply reporting activity.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Governance reporting should answer four questions:

1. What risks exist?
2. How effectively are we managing them?
3. Where are governance weaknesses emerging?
4. What requires leadership attention?

The purpose of governance metrics is not measurement alone.

The purpose is to support decision making.

---

# Governance Reporting Categories

The organization monitors governance performance through six reporting domains:

1. Risk Management Metrics
2. Issue Management Metrics
3. Remediation Metrics
4. Vendor Risk Metrics
5. Policy Exception Metrics
6. Program Effectiveness Metrics

---

# Risk Management Metrics

## Open Risks

Measures total active risks currently tracked within the enterprise risk register.

Formula:

```text
Count of Active Risks
```

Purpose:

- Measures overall risk inventory
- Supports risk trending analysis

---

## High Risk Count

Measures total risks classified as High Risk.

Formula:

```text
Count of High Risks
```

Purpose:

- Measures significant organizational exposure
- Supports executive prioritization

---

## Risk Severity Distribution

Measures risk concentration by severity.

Example:

| Severity | Count |
|----------|----------|
| Low | 25 |
| Moderate | 18 |
| High | 7 |

Purpose:

- Identifies concentration of risk exposure

---

## Average Risk Score

Measures average enterprise risk level.

Formula:

```text
Sum of Risk Scores
÷
Total Risks
```

Purpose:

- Tracks overall organizational risk posture

---

# Issue Management Metrics

## Open Issues

Measures total unresolved governance issues.

Formula:

```text
Count of Open Issues
```

Purpose:

- Measures governance workload

---

## High Severity Issues

Measures unresolved High Severity findings.

Purpose:

- Supports leadership visibility

---

## Overdue Issues

Measures issues exceeding approved SLA requirements.

Formula:

```text
Count of Overdue Issues
```

Purpose:

- Measures governance effectiveness

---

## Issue Closure Rate

Formula:

```text
Closed Issues
÷
Total Issues
× 100
```

Purpose:

- Measures issue resolution effectiveness

---

# Remediation Metrics

## Open Remediation Activities

Measures active remediation efforts.

Purpose:

- Tracks remediation workload

---

## Remediation Completion Rate

Formula:

```text
Completed Remediations
÷
Total Remediations
× 100
```

Purpose:

- Measures remediation effectiveness

---

## Average Time to Remediate

Formula:

```text
Total Days to Close
÷
Closed Remediations
```

Purpose:

- Measures remediation efficiency

---

## Overdue Remediation Rate

Formula:

```text
Overdue Remediations
÷
Open Remediations
× 100
```

Purpose:

- Identifies execution challenges

# Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

The organization monitors KRIs to identify increasing risk exposure.

Examples:

| KRI | Threshold |
|----------|----------|
| High Risks | >10 |
| Overdue High Risks | >5 |
| Critical Vendor Findings | >3 |
| Failed Validations | >10% |
| Overdue Remediation | >15% |

Thresholds should trigger governance review and escalation.

---

# Executive Dashboard Metrics

Leadership dashboards should focus on:

- Total Open Risks
- High Risks
- Open Issues
- Overdue Remediation
- Critical Vendors
- Vendor Findings
- Active Exceptions
- SLA Compliance Rate
- Audit Readiness Status

The objective is to provide leadership with a concise view of organizational risk posture.

# Audit Readiness Considerations

Governance metrics should be:

- Consistently calculated
- Documented
- Repeatable
- Traceable to source data
- Retained for audit review

# Summary

The Governance KPIs & Metrics Framework establishes a standardized approach for measuring risk, governance effectiveness, remediation performance, vendor oversight, policy compliance, and overall IRM program maturity.

By providing actionable metrics and executive visibility, the organization strengthens governance decision making, accountability, and enterprise risk management effectiveness.
