# Executive Dashboard Model

## Overview

The Executive Dashboard Model establishes the governance framework for presenting enterprise risk, compliance, remediation, audit readiness, and third-party risk information to executive leadership through centralized dashboard reporting.

The purpose of executive dashboards is to:

- Provide leadership visibility into organizational risk posture
- Support risk-based decision making
- Improve governance oversight
- Track remediation performance
- Monitor regulatory exposure
- Support audit readiness
- Enable proactive risk management

The dashboard serves as a centralized governance reporting platform that consolidates information from risk management, issue management, vendor risk management, policy governance, and compliance activities.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Executives should not need to review:

- Individual findings
- Detailed assessments
- Technical control implementations
- Operational task tracking

Executives should understand:

- What risks exist
- What risks require action
- How effectively risk is being managed
- Where governance weaknesses exist
- What decisions require leadership involvement

The dashboard should transform governance data into actionable business intelligence.

---

# Dashboard Architecture

The Executive Dashboard consists of six primary reporting domains:

```text
Enterprise Risk
       │
Issue Management
       │
Remediation Performance
       │
Third-Party Risk
       │
Compliance & Audit Readiness
       │
Governance Program Health
```

# Summary

The Executive Dashboard Model establishes a centralized governance reporting framework that provides leadership with visibility into enterprise risk, compliance posture, remediation performance, third-party risk exposure, and overall governance effectiveness.

By consolidating governance information into actionable executive reporting, leadership can make informed decisions that support organizational objectives, regulatory compliance, and enterprise resilience.
