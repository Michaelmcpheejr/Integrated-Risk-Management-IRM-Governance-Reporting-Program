# Issue Management Lifecycle

## Overview

The Issue Management Lifecycle defines the centralized governance workflow used to identify, classify, assign, remediate, validate, escalate, and close governance-related issues across the organization.

The purpose of the lifecycle is to ensure:
- governance issues are centrally tracked
- remediation ownership is established
- accountability is measurable
- escalation procedures are standardized
- validation activities are documented
- executive visibility is maintained

The lifecycle supports enterprise governance operations aligned to Integrated Risk Management (IRM) principles and enterprise GRC operating models.

---

# Enterprise Issue Management Lifecycle

The issue management lifecycle operates through the following governance stages:

1. Issue Identification  
2. Issue Intake & Classification  
3. Severity & Impact Assessment  
4. Ownership Assignment  
5. Remediation Planning  
6. Remediation Execution  
7. Validation & Governance Review  
8. Escalation Management  
9. Residual Risk Evaluation  
10. Issue Closure  
11. Continuous Monitoring & Reporting  

Each stage supports centralized governance coordination and workflow traceability.

---

# 1. Issue Identification

Governance issues may be identified through:
- audit activities
- security assessments
- compliance reviews
- vulnerability management
- policy reviews
- vendor governance activities
- operational governance reviews
- control testing
- incident investigations

Issue identification ensures governance concerns are formally documented and entered into centralized governance workflows.

---

# 2. Issue Intake & Classification

Once identified, governance issues undergo centralized intake and classification procedures.

Issues are categorized based on:
- issue type
- severity
- business impact
- compliance exposure
- affected systems
- operational criticality
- ownership requirements

Issue categories may include:
- audit findings
- security control failures
- policy exceptions
- compliance deficiencies
- remediation gaps
- vendor governance findings
- operational governance issues

Classification activities support governance consistency and standardized reporting.

---

# 3. Severity & Impact Assessment

Issues are formally assessed to determine:
- operational impact
- business exposure
- regulatory implications
- remediation urgency
- escalation requirements

Severity assessments incorporate:
- likelihood
- business impact
- asset criticality
- control maturity
- detectability
- compliance exposure

Issue severity levels support:
- SLA assignment
- remediation prioritization
- governance reporting
- escalation workflows

---

# Severity Model

| Severity | Description |
|---|---|
| Critical | Material governance exposure requiring immediate escalation |
| High | Significant operational or compliance risk requiring prioritized remediation |
| Medium | Moderate governance exposure requiring scheduled remediation |
| Low | Limited operational impact with manageable governance exposure |

---

# 4. Ownership Assignment

Governance ownership is formally assigned to:
- remediation owners
- business stakeholders
- asset owners
- governance reviewers
- validation teams

Ownership assignment establishes accountability for:
- remediation execution
- status reporting
- evidence collection
- issue coordination
- escalation response

Assigned stakeholders are responsible for maintaining governance workflow updates throughout remediation activities.

---

# 5. Remediation Planning

Stakeholders develop remediation plans designed to:
- resolve governance gaps
- reduce operational exposure
- improve control effectiveness
- mitigate compliance risk
- strengthen governance maturity

Remediation plans include:
- corrective actions
- ownership assignments
- target completion dates
- validation requirements
- escalation thresholds
- residual risk considerations

All remediation activities remain centrally traceable within governance workflows.

---

# 6. Remediation Execution

Assigned stakeholders execute remediation activities according to:
- governance SLAs
- issue severity
- operational criticality
- compliance obligations
- remediation timelines

Governance oversight activities include:
- remediation tracking
- issue aging analysis
- overdue remediation monitoring
- escalation management
- governance reporting updates

High-risk remediation activities may require enhanced governance oversight and executive visibility.

---

# 7. Validation & Governance Review

Completed remediation activities undergo formal validation procedures performed by:
- GRC reviewers
- Security teams
- compliance stakeholders
- operational reviewers

Validation activities confirm:
- remediation effectiveness
- issue resolution
- control implementation
- evidence completeness
- governance alignment

Validation failures may require:
- remediation reopening
- escalation review
- compensating controls
- residual risk analysis

Validation workflows improve governance defensibility and audit readiness.

---

# 8. Escalation Management

The issue management lifecycle incorporates formal governance escalation procedures designed to address:
- overdue remediation
- unresolved high-risk findings
- repeated control failures
- SLA violations
- elevated compliance exposure
- material operational risk

Escalation decisions are based on:
- severity
- business criticality
- regulatory impact
- unresolved remediation timelines
- residual risk exposure

Escalated issues may require:
- governance committee review
- executive oversight
- remediation prioritization
- formal risk acceptance decisions

---

# Escalation Levels

| Escalation Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Level 1 | Operational remediation management |
| Level 2 | GRC oversight escalation |
| Level 3 | Cross-functional governance review |
| Level 4 | Executive leadership escalation |
| Level 5 | Formal enterprise risk acceptance review |

---

# 9. Residual Risk Evaluation

Issues that cannot be fully remediated undergo residual risk evaluation procedures.

Residual risk reviews assess:
- remaining exposure
- compensating controls
- business constraints
- operational limitations
- regulatory implications

Residual risks requiring formal acceptance must include:
- documented business justification
- governance review
- executive approval
- centralized tracking
- periodic reassessment

Residual risk visibility is maintained through governance reporting workflows.

---

# 10. Issue Closure

Issues may only be formally closed after:
- remediation completion
- successful validation
- evidence review
- governance approval
- residual risk documentation (if applicable)

Issue closure activities ensure governance records remain:
- accurate
- defensible
- auditable
- centrally traceable

Closed issues remain available for:
- governance reporting
- audit reviews
- trend analysis
- historical governance metrics

---

# 11. Continuous Monitoring & Reporting

Issue management operations support continuous governance monitoring activities including:
- remediation tracking
- SLA monitoring
- issue aging reviews
- trend analysis
- escalation reporting
- governance KPI analysis
- audit readiness reviews

Layered governance reporting supports:
- operational teams
- governance management
- executive leadership
- governance committees

Continuous monitoring improves governance maturity and remediation accountability.

---

# SLA Governance Integration

Issue management workflows integrate severity-based SLA governance models.

SLA requirements are based on:
- issue severity
- business criticality
- regulatory exposure
- operational impact

SLA violations automatically trigger governance escalation procedures and enhanced oversight activities.

---

# Governance Reporting Integration

Issue management reporting supports:
- remediation visibility
- governance oversight
- executive awareness
- audit traceability
- operational accountability

Governance reporting includes:
- open issues
- overdue remediation
- SLA performance
- escalation trends
- issue aging
- residual risk exposure

---

# Issue Management Outcomes

The Issue Management Lifecycle is designed to:
- centralize governance issue operations
- improve remediation accountability
- strengthen governance oversight
- improve workflow traceability
- support executive visibility
- enhance audit readiness
- improve governance maturity

The lifecycle establishes a repeatable enterprise issue governance model aligned to Integrated Risk Management (IRM) operational concepts.
