# IRM Operating Model

## Overview

The Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Operating Model defines how governance, risk, compliance, remediation, and executive oversight activities operate within the organization.

The purpose of the operating model is to establish:
- centralized governance coordination
- risk accountability
- remediation oversight
- enterprise traceability
- layered reporting visibility
- consistent governance operations

The operating model is designed using a hybrid governance approach that balances:
- compliance obligations
- operational risk management
- remediation accountability
- executive risk visibility

This model aligns to enterprise governance concepts commonly associated with modern GRC and ServiceNow IRM operating structures.

---

# Governance Philosophy

The organization operates under a hybrid governance philosophy focused on balancing:
- regulatory compliance
- operational risk reduction
- remediation execution
- business continuity
- executive oversight

Governance activities are designed to ensure that:
- risks are identifiable and measurable
- ownership is formally assigned
- remediation activities are traceable
- unresolved issues are escalated appropriately
- leadership maintains visibility into enterprise risk posture

The operating model prioritizes:
- centralized governance coordination
- cross-functional accountability
- risk-based decision making
- repeatable governance workflows
- audit-ready operational processes

# Governance Functional Structure

## Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

The GRC function serves as the central governance coordinator responsible for:
- operating IRM workflows
- managing risk lifecycle operations
- facilitating issue management
- tracking remediation progress
- maintaining governance reporting
- coordinating risk reviews
- supporting audit readiness activities

The GRC function also maintains centralized governance documentation and reporting consistency across business and technology environments.

---

## Security Operations

Security teams support the operating model through:
- technical validation of findings
- control effectiveness reviews
- remediation verification
- escalation support
- security risk analysis

Security teams provide subject matter expertise for technical governance decisions and high-risk remediation activities.

---

## Compliance & Legal

Compliance and Legal functions provide guidance relating to:
- regulatory obligations
- policy alignment
- compliance risk exposure
- audit expectations
- governance documentation requirements

These teams assist in evaluating legal and regulatory impact associated with unresolved risks and policy exceptions.

---

## Business & Asset Owners

Business owners are responsible for:
- operational risk ownership
- remediation execution
- issue response coordination
- validation support
- risk treatment activities

Asset owners are accountable for maintaining governance alignment within systems, applications, and operational processes under their responsibility.

---

## Executive Leadership

Executive leadership provides:
- governance oversight
- strategic risk direction
- residual risk approval
- escalation review
- remediation accountability enforcement

Leadership maintains visibility into enterprise risk posture through layered governance reporting and executive dashboards.

# Enterprise Risk Lifecycle

The IRM lifecycle follows a formal enterprise governance workflow consisting of:

1. Risk Identification  
2. Risk Assessment  
3. Weighted Risk Scoring  
4. Ownership Assignment  
5. Risk Treatment Planning  
6. Remediation Execution  
7. Validation & Review  
8. Residual Risk Analysis  
9. Risk Acceptance Decision  
10. Continuous Monitoring  

Each stage of the lifecycle requires documented governance activities, ownership accountability, and centralized workflow tracking.

---

# Enterprise Risk Scoring Model

The operating model utilizes a weighted governance scoring methodology incorporating:
- likelihood
- business impact
- control maturity
- detectability
- business criticality
- residual exposure

Weighted scoring improves governance prioritization by aligning technical and operational risks with business impact and executive decision-making requirements.

- ownership assignment

# Enterprise Governance Escalation Model

The operating model utilizes an enterprise governance escalation framework based on:
- severity
- business criticality
- regulatory exposure
- SLA violations
- unresolved remediation timelines
- residual risk impact

Escalation workflows support timely governance intervention for:
- overdue remediation activities
- high-risk findings
- unresolved control failures
- elevated compliance exposure
- material governance risks

Escalated issues may require:
- executive review
- governance committee oversight
- formal risk acceptance decisions
- compensating control implementation

---

# Residual Risk Governance

Residual risks that remain after remediation activities are subject to formal governance review.

Residual risk governance requires:
- documented business justification
- analysis of remaining exposure
- identification of compensating controls
- formal approval workflows
- centralized governance tracking
- periodic review cadence

Residual risk acceptance decisions are maintained as part of the enterprise governance record and remain visible within executive reporting workflows.
