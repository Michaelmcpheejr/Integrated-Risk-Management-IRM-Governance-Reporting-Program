# Governance Structure

## Overview

The Governance Structure defines the organizational oversight model supporting the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The purpose of the governance structure is to establish:
- accountability
- governance ownership
- escalation authority
- remediation oversight
- executive visibility
- cross-functional coordination

The structure is designed to support centralized governance operations while ensuring business, security, compliance, and leadership stakeholders maintain clearly defined responsibilities throughout the risk lifecycle.

---

# Governance Operating Principles

The governance structure operates using the following principles:

- centralized governance coordination
- risk-based decision making
- layered accountability
- formal escalation procedures
- remediation ownership
- executive oversight
- audit traceability
- continuous governance operations

Governance decisions are supported through documented workflows, centralized reporting, and structured review processes.

---

# Governance Organizational Model

The governance model utilizes a layered enterprise governance structure consisting of:
- operational governance
- management governance
- executive governance

This layered structure supports both day-to-day governance operations and strategic enterprise oversight.

---

# Governance Functional Roles

## Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

### Primary Responsibilities
- Operate IRM governance workflows
- Maintain centralized risk register
- Coordinate issue management activities
- Track remediation efforts
- Facilitate governance reporting
- Manage escalation workflows
- Support audit readiness operations
- Maintain governance documentation

### Governance Authority
The GRC function serves as the centralized governance coordination body responsible for ensuring governance workflows operate consistently across the organization.

The GRC function is responsible for:
- workflow orchestration
- governance reporting consistency
- remediation oversight coordination
- residual risk governance tracking
- issue lifecycle management

---

## Security Operations

### Primary Responsibilities
- Validate technical findings
- Assess control effectiveness
- Support remediation validation
- Investigate security-related governance issues
- Provide technical risk analysis
- Support escalation reviews

### Governance Authority
Security teams provide subject matter expertise for:
- technical risk validation
- security control analysis
- remediation verification
- security impact assessments

Security functions support GRC governance decisions through technical review and validation processes.

---

## Compliance & Legal

### Primary Responsibilities
- Interpret regulatory obligations
- Review policy alignment
- Assess compliance exposure
- Support governance documentation requirements
- Advise on legal and regulatory risk

### Governance Authority
Compliance and Legal functions provide governance advisory support relating to:
- HIPAA
- SOX ITGC
- ISO 27001
- NIST governance alignment
- policy exception reviews
- audit response activities

---

## Business Owners

### Primary Responsibilities
- Own operational risks
- Coordinate remediation activities
- Validate business impact
- Support issue resolution
- Participate in risk treatment planning

### Governance Authority
Business owners are accountable for:
- remediation execution
- operational governance alignment
- risk response coordination
- issue ownership

Business stakeholders are expected to participate in governance review activities impacting their operational environments.

---

## Asset Owners

### Primary Responsibilities
- Maintain governance alignment for assigned systems
- Coordinate technical remediation activities
- Support control validation
- Participate in issue management workflows
- Assist with audit evidence collection

### Governance Authority
Asset owners maintain accountability for governance-related activities impacting:
- applications
- infrastructure
- business systems
- technology platforms
- operational assets

---

## Executive Leadership

### Primary Responsibilities
- Review enterprise governance reporting
- Approve residual risk acceptance
- Review escalated high-risk issues
- Support strategic governance decisions
- Monitor enterprise risk posture

### Governance Authority
Executive leadership maintains authority relating to:
- enterprise risk acceptance
- unresolved high-risk remediation activities
- governance prioritization
- enterprise governance direction

Executive leadership receives layered governance reporting designed to support risk-informed decision making.

# Governance Escalation Structure

The governance escalation structure supports timely escalation of:
- unresolved high-risk findings
- overdue remediation activities
- SLA violations
- regulatory exposure
- control failures
- audit readiness concerns

Escalation decisions are based on:
- severity
- business impact
- compliance exposure
- remediation delays
- residual risk exposure

# Residual Risk Governance

Residual risks that remain after remediation activities require:
- documented business justification
- compensating control identification
- formal governance review
- executive approval
- centralized tracking
- periodic reassessment

Residual risk decisions are incorporated into executive governance reporting workflows.
