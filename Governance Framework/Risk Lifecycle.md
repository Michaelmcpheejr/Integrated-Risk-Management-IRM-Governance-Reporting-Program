# Risk Lifecycle

## Overview

The Risk Lifecycle defines the standardized enterprise workflow used to identify, assess, prioritize, remediate, monitor, and govern risks throughout the organization.

The purpose of the lifecycle is to ensure:
- risks are centrally traceable
- ownership is formally assigned
- remediation activities are measurable
- governance decisions are documented
- executive visibility is maintained
- residual risks are properly governed

The lifecycle supports repeatable governance operations aligned to enterprise Integrated Risk Management (IRM) concepts.

---

# Enterprise Risk Lifecycle Model

The enterprise risk lifecycle operates through the following governance stages:

1. Risk Identification  
2. Risk Intake & Classification  
3. Risk Assessment  
4. Weighted Risk Scoring  
5. Ownership Assignment  
6. Risk Treatment Planning  
7. Remediation Execution  
8. Validation & Governance Review  
9. Residual Risk Analysis  
10. Risk Acceptance & Escalation  
11. Continuous Monitoring  

Each stage supports centralized governance oversight and formal workflow traceability.

---

# 1. Risk Identification

The lifecycle begins when risks are identified through operational, security, compliance, audit, or third-party governance activities.

Risk sources may include:
- security assessments
- audit findings
- vulnerability reviews
- control failures
- compliance gaps
- policy exceptions
- vendor assessments
- operational governance reviews
- incident investigations

The objective of this stage is to ensure risks are formally documented and entered into centralized governance workflows.

---

# 2. Risk Intake & Classification

Identified risks undergo centralized intake and classification procedures.

Risks are categorized based on:
- risk type
- affected business process
- regulatory impact
- operational criticality
- asset exposure
- business function
- governance ownership

Example risk categories may include:
- operational risk
- security risk
- compliance risk
- vendor risk
- audit risk
- technology risk
- policy governance risk

Classification supports governance consistency and reporting standardization.

---

# 3. Risk Assessment

Governance stakeholders perform formal risk assessments to evaluate:
- potential business impact
- operational exposure
- regulatory implications
- control effectiveness
- likelihood of occurrence
- affected business assets

Risk assessments are supported through collaboration between:
- GRC teams
- Security operations
- business stakeholders
- compliance teams
- asset owners

Assessment activities establish the foundation for governance prioritization and remediation planning.

---

# 4. Weighted Risk Scoring

The operating model utilizes a weighted enterprise scoring methodology designed to support risk-informed governance decisions.

Scoring incorporates:
- likelihood
- business impact
- control maturity
- detectability
- business criticality
- residual exposure

Weighted scoring supports:
- governance prioritization
- remediation urgency
- escalation decisions
- reporting visibility
- executive oversight

Scoring outputs determine whether risks require:
- operational remediation
- enhanced governance oversight
- executive escalation
- formal risk acceptance review

---

# 5. Ownership Assignment

Governance ownership is formally assigned following risk scoring activities.

Assigned governance roles may include:
- business owner
- asset owner
- remediation owner
- governance reviewer
- executive approver

Ownership assignment establishes accountability for:
- remediation execution
- risk treatment coordination
- governance reporting updates
- validation support
- escalation management

All ownership activities are centrally documented within governance workflows.

---

# 6. Risk Treatment Planning

Governance stakeholders develop treatment plans designed to reduce or manage identified risks.

Risk treatment activities may include:
- control implementation
- remediation activities
- process improvements
- policy enhancements
- compensating controls
- vendor remediation
- governance process changes

Treatment plans include:
- remediation actions
- ownership assignments
- target completion dates
- validation requirements
- escalation thresholds

Treatment planning supports measurable governance operations and remediation accountability.

---

# 7. Remediation Execution

Assigned stakeholders execute remediation activities according to:
- governance SLAs
- risk severity
- operational criticality
- regulatory exposure
- business impact

Remediation workflows remain centrally monitored throughout execution.

Governance oversight activities include:
- remediation tracking
- issue aging analysis
- overdue remediation reviews
- escalation monitoring
- governance reporting updates

High-risk remediation efforts may require enhanced governance oversight and executive visibility.

---

# 8. Validation & Governance Review

Completed remediation efforts undergo formal validation procedures.

Validation activities may be performed by:
- GRC reviewers
- Security operations
- compliance stakeholders
- asset owners
- governance management

Validation procedures confirm:
- remediation effectiveness
- control implementation
- issue resolution
- governance alignment
- evidence completeness

If remediation efforts fail validation:
- remediation may be reopened
- escalation workflows may activate
- compensating controls may be required
- residual risk analysis may begin

Validation activities maintain governance defensibility and operational consistency.

---

# 9. Residual Risk Analysis

Residual risks remaining after remediation are formally evaluated through governance review workflows.

Residual risk analysis evaluates:
- remaining exposure
- control limitations
- operational constraints
- business impact
- compensating controls
- governance implications

Residual risk reviews determine whether remaining exposure:
- is acceptable
- requires additional remediation
- requires executive escalation
- requires compensating controls

Residual risk governance ensures unresolved exposure remains visible and formally documented.

---

# 10. Risk Acceptance & Escalation

Risks exceeding defined governance thresholds may require formal escalation and executive review.

Governance escalation decisions are based on:
- severity
- regulatory exposure
- unresolved remediation
- SLA violations
- business criticality
- residual exposure

Executive governance reviews may include:
- formal risk acceptance
- remediation extensions
- compensating control approval
- governance prioritization decisions
- strategic remediation directives

Risk acceptance decisions require:
- documented business justification
- executive approval
- centralized governance tracking
- review cadence assignment

All accepted risks remain visible through governance reporting workflows.

---

# 11. Continuous Monitoring

The risk lifecycle supports continuous governance monitoring activities designed to maintain long-term visibility into enterprise risk posture.

Continuous monitoring activities include:
- recurring risk reviews
- remediation performance tracking
- governance KPI analysis
- SLA monitoring
- issue trend analysis
- control effectiveness reviews
- vendor governance monitoring
- audit readiness assessments

Continuous monitoring improves:
- governance maturity
- remediation accountability
- operational visibility
- executive oversight
- enterprise traceability

---

# Risk Escalation Integration

Risk escalation workflows are integrated throughout the lifecycle.

Escalation may occur due to:
- unresolved high-risk findings
- overdue remediation activities
- repeated control failures
- elevated compliance exposure
- inadequate remediation progress
- material business impact

Escalated risks receive enhanced governance oversight and executive visibility where required.

---

# Risk Reporting Integration

Risk reporting activities support layered governance visibility across:
- operational governance teams
- GRC management
- compliance leadership
- executive leadership
- governance committees

Reporting visibility includes:
- open risks
- remediation status
- residual risk exposure
- SLA performance
- issue aging
- governance trends
- executive risk summaries

---

# Risk Lifecycle Outcomes

The Risk Lifecycle is designed to:
- improve governance consistency
- centralize enterprise risk operations
- strengthen remediation accountability
- improve governance traceability
- support executive oversight
- improve audit readiness
- enhance enterprise governance maturity

The lifecycle establishes a scalable governance framework aligned to enterprise Integrated Risk Management (IRM) operational concepts.
