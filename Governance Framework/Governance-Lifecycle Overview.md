# Governance Lifecycle Overview

## Overview

The Governance Lifecycle Overview defines the centralized operational flow supporting the Integrated Risk Management (IRM) Governance & Reporting Program.

The lifecycle establishes how governance activities move through:
- identification
- assessment
- prioritization
- remediation
- validation
- escalation
- residual risk review
- continuous monitoring

The objective of the lifecycle is to ensure governance activities remain:
- traceable
- repeatable
- centrally governed
- measurable
- audit-ready

The lifecycle supports enterprise governance operations across:
- risk management
- issue management
- remediation governance
- audit readiness
- compliance operations
- third-party risk oversight

---

# Enterprise Governance Lifecycle

The enterprise governance lifecycle operates through ten centralized governance stages:

1. Risk & Issue Identification  
2. Governance Intake & Classification  
3. Risk Assessment & Scoring  
4. Ownership Assignment  
5. Treatment & Remediation Planning  
6. Remediation Execution  
7. Validation & Governance Review  
8. Residual Risk Analysis  
9. Executive Review & Risk Acceptance  
10. Continuous Monitoring & Reporting  

Each governance stage supports centralized oversight and documented workflow traceability.

---

# 1. Risk & Issue Identification

Governance activities begin when risks, issues, findings, or control gaps are identified through operational, security, compliance, audit, or third-party governance activities.

Sources may include:
- security assessments
- audit findings
- control failures
- compliance reviews
- vulnerability management
- vendor assessments
- policy exceptions
- operational governance reviews

The objective of this stage is to ensure governance concerns are formally documented and entered into centralized governance workflows.

---

# 2. Governance Intake & Classification

Once identified, governance items are classified based on:
- issue type
- business impact
- operational criticality
- regulatory exposure
- affected assets
- ownership requirements

Items are categorized into governance workflows such as:
- enterprise risks
- security findings
- audit issues
- policy exceptions
- vendor governance issues
- remediation requests

This stage establishes governance consistency and centralized intake management.

---

# 3. Risk Assessment & Scoring

Governance items undergo formal assessment using the enterprise weighted scoring methodology.

Assessment criteria include:
- likelihood
- business impact
- control maturity
- detectability
- business criticality
- residual exposure

Scoring results determine:
- remediation urgency
- escalation requirements
- reporting visibility
- governance prioritization

Risk scoring supports risk-informed governance decision-making.

---

# 4. Ownership Assignment

Governance ownership is formally assigned to:
- business owners
- asset owners
- remediation owners
- validation stakeholders
- governance reviewers

Ownership assignment establishes accountability for:
- remediation execution
- issue coordination
- validation support
- governance reporting updates

Escalation paths are also defined during this stage.

---

# 5. Treatment & Remediation Planning

Governance stakeholders develop remediation and treatment plans designed to:
- reduce operational risk
- improve control effectiveness
- resolve governance gaps
- mitigate compliance exposure

Treatment planning may include:
- control implementation
- process improvements
- technical remediation
- policy updates
- compensating controls
- vendor remediation activities

Planned remediation activities are tracked through centralized governance workflows.

---

# 6. Remediation Execution

Assigned stakeholders execute remediation activities according to:
- governance SLAs
- remediation timelines
- business criticality
- operational priorities

Remediation workflows remain centrally tracked throughout execution.

Governance monitoring includes:
- remediation status reviews
- overdue remediation tracking
- escalation monitoring
- issue aging analysis

High-risk remediation activities may require enhanced governance oversight.

---

# 7. Validation & Governance Review

Completed remediation activities undergo formal validation by:
- GRC reviewers
- Security teams
- compliance stakeholders
- operational reviewers

Validation activities confirm:
- remediation effectiveness
- control implementation
- issue resolution
- governance alignment

Failed validation activities may require:
- remediation reopening
- escalation review
- compensating controls
- residual risk analysis

---

# 8. Residual Risk Analysis

Residual risks remaining after remediation efforts are formally evaluated.

Residual risk reviews assess:
- remaining exposure
- control limitations
- business impact
- compensating controls
- operational constraints

Residual risks requiring acceptance are escalated through governance review workflows.

Residual risk decisions are documented and maintained within centralized governance records.

---

# 9. Executive Review & Risk Acceptance

High-risk governance items requiring executive oversight are reviewed through formal governance escalation processes.

Executive review activities may include:
- residual risk acceptance
- remediation extension approvals
- governance prioritization decisions
- policy exception approvals
- strategic remediation direction

Executive governance decisions are formally documented and incorporated into enterprise reporting workflows.

---

# 10. Continuous Monitoring & Reporting

Governance operations continue through ongoing monitoring and reporting activities.

Continuous governance monitoring includes:
- SLA tracking
- remediation performance
- issue trend analysis
- governance KPI reviews
- control effectiveness monitoring
- vendor governance monitoring
- audit readiness reviews

Layered governance reporting supports:
- operational teams
- governance management
- executive leadership
- governance committees

Continuous monitoring ensures governance operations remain measurable, traceable, and aligned to enterprise risk priorities.

---

# Governance Workflow Relationships

The governance lifecycle maintains centralized relationships between:
- risks
- controls
- issues
- remediation tasks
- policies
- business assets
- stakeholders
- governance decisions

This relationship model improves:
- enterprise traceability
- governance visibility
- remediation accountability
- reporting consistency
- audit defensibility

---

# Governance Escalation Integration

Governance escalation workflows are integrated throughout the lifecycle based on:
- severity
- SLA violations
- regulatory exposure
- remediation delays
- unresolved high-risk findings
- residual risk exposure

Escalation procedures ensure governance issues receive appropriate oversight and executive visibility when required.

---

# Governance Reporting Integration

Governance reporting is integrated throughout the lifecycle to provide:
- operational workflow visibility
- governance oversight metrics
- remediation status reporting
- executive risk summaries
- audit readiness visibility

Reporting activities support centralized governance coordination and enterprise risk visibility.

---

# Lifecycle Outcomes

The Governance Lifecycle Overview is designed to:
- centralize governance operations
- improve workflow consistency
- strengthen accountability
- improve remediation oversight
- support executive visibility
- enhance audit readiness
- improve governance maturity

The lifecycle establishes a repeatable enterprise governance operating model aligned to Integrated Risk Management (IRM) principles.
