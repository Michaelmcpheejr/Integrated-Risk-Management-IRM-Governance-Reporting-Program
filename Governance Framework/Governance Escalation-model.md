# Governance Escalation Model

## Overview

The Governance Escalation Model defines the formal escalation structure used to manage unresolved governance issues, overdue remediation activities, elevated risk exposure, and material compliance concerns throughout the organization.

The purpose of the escalation model is to ensure:
- governance accountability is maintained
- high-risk issues receive appropriate oversight
- unresolved remediation activities are escalated consistently
- executive leadership maintains visibility into material governance exposure
- escalation decisions remain centrally traceable

The escalation model supports enterprise governance operations aligned to Integrated Risk Management (IRM) principles and enterprise GRC operating models.

---

# Escalation Governance Principles

The governance escalation model operates using the following principles:

- risk-based escalation
- centralized governance oversight
- layered accountability
- severity-driven prioritization
- measurable remediation accountability
- executive visibility
- audit traceability
- formal governance review procedures

Escalation activities are designed to ensure unresolved governance exposure receives appropriate operational, management, and executive attention.

---

# Escalation Triggers

Governance escalation may occur when:
- remediation activities exceed SLA thresholds
- high-risk findings remain unresolved
- critical control failures are identified
- residual risk exceeds governance tolerance
- repeated policy violations occur
- compliance obligations are not met
- audit findings remain unresolved
- vendor remediation efforts fail
- operational exposure materially increases

Escalation workflows ensure governance concerns remain visible until resolution or formal acceptance.

---

# Escalation Severity Model

Governance escalation activities are prioritized using the enterprise severity model.

| Severity | Description |
|---|---|
| Critical | Material enterprise risk requiring immediate executive visibility |
| High | Significant governance exposure requiring prioritized remediation |
| Medium | Moderate governance exposure requiring management oversight |
| Low | Limited governance impact with manageable operational exposure |

Severity ratings influence:
- escalation timelines
- reporting visibility
- remediation urgency
- governance oversight requirements
- executive review thresholds

# Enterprise Escalation Levels

## Level 1 — Operational Remediation Management

### Purpose
Supports day-to-day remediation coordination and issue ownership management.

### Ownership
- remediation owners
- operational stakeholders
- asset owners

### Activities
- remediation tracking
- workflow coordination
- status reporting
- issue updates
- operational validation

### Escalation Thresholds
Escalation to Level 2 occurs when:
- remediation timelines are missed
- remediation progress stalls
- issue severity increases
- operational impact expands

---

## Level 2 — GRC Oversight Escalation

### Purpose
Provides centralized governance oversight for unresolved or elevated governance issues.

### Ownership
- GRC management
- governance coordinators
- issue management stakeholders

### Activities
- governance review
- remediation oversight
- SLA monitoring
- escalation coordination
- governance reporting updates

### Escalation Thresholds
Escalation to Level 3 occurs when:
- SLA violations continue
- remediation efforts fail
- governance exposure increases
- cross-functional coordination becomes necessary

---

## Level 3 — Cross-Functional Governance Review

### Purpose
Supports enterprise coordination for governance issues requiring multiple stakeholder groups.

### Participants
- GRC leadership
- Security operations
- Compliance representatives
- business stakeholders
- operational management

### Activities
- governance review meetings
- remediation prioritization
- cross-functional coordination
- control gap analysis
- compensating control evaluation

### Escalation Thresholds
Escalation to Level 4 occurs when:
- governance exposure becomes material
- regulatory impact increases
- remediation remains unresolved
- executive oversight becomes necessary

---

## Level 4 — Executive Governance Escalation

### Purpose
Provides executive leadership visibility into unresolved high-risk governance exposure.

### Participants
- executive leadership
- governance leadership
- security leadership
- compliance leadership

### Activities
- executive governance reviews
- remediation prioritization decisions
- enterprise risk discussions
- governance oversight actions
- strategic remediation direction

### Escalation Thresholds
Escalation to Level 5 occurs when:
- residual risk exceeds governance tolerance
- remediation cannot be completed
- business constraints prevent resolution
- formal risk acceptance becomes necessary

---

## Level 5 — Enterprise Risk Acceptance Review

### Purpose
Supports formal governance review and acceptance of unresolved residual risk.

### Participants
- executive leadership
- governance committees
- business leadership
- GRC leadership
- compliance stakeholders

### Activities
- residual risk review
- compensating control analysis
- formal risk acceptance decisions
- governance documentation review
- reassessment scheduling

### Governance Requirements
Formal risk acceptance requires:
- documented business justification
- analysis of unresolved exposure
- compensating control identification
- executive approval
- centralized governance tracking
- periodic reassessment cadence

Accepted risks remain visible within governance reporting workflows.
